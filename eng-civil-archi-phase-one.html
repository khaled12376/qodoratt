Program Overview

This foundational phase establishes the core technical and managerial knowledge for a modern civil and architectural engineer. It focuses on essential concepts in engineering management, architectural and structural design fundamentals, and the basics of technical office operations. The phase is crucial for building a strong base in quality and safety management practices.

Key Topics Covered

  • Engineering Management Fundamentals
  • Architectural Design Principles
  • Structural Design Basics
  • Technical Office Operations
  • Quality Management Practices
  • Safety Management Systems
  • Project Documentation and Coordination
  • Building Codes and Regulations

Learning Outcomes

Upon completion of Phase One, participants will be able to:

  • Apply engineering management principles to construction projects
  • Understand and interpret architectural and structural drawings
  • Perform basic technical office operations and documentation
  • Implement quality assurance procedures in construction
  • Apply safety standards and regulations on construction sites
  • Coordinate between different engineering disciplines
