Program Overview

Our Procurement & Supply Chain Programs provide comprehensive training in modern procurement practices, supply chain management, and warehouse operations. Master strategic sourcing, contract management, risk mitigation, and build effective procurement strategies that align with organizational goals and optimize supply chain performance. Our curriculum covers 6 specialized programs designed to develop procurement and supply chain professionals at all levels.

Duration

6 comprehensive programs
Flexible scheduling available

Certification

Procurement Professional Certificate
Recognized Accreditation

Our 6 Specialized Procurement & Supply Chain Programs

1. Local and Foreign Procurement Policies and Procedures Systems

Objectives:

Identify the integrated information and systems through local and foreign procurement policies and procedures systems and their technical aspects.

Main Axes:
  • Fundamentals of procurement practices (procurement policies)
  • Methods of internal and foreign procurement – and selection of suppliers
  • Strategies for setting and discussing prices for materials and after-sales services
  • Modern strategies in preparing and drafting administrative contracts
  • Addressing linguistic errors in contract drafting and avoiding potential legal problems
  • Planning for negotiation in procurement activities
  • Tactics and methods for selecting procurement operations for negotiation and concluding deals
  • Practical lab on negotiation and bargaining processes for procurement operations
Enroll in This Program

2. Public Procurement Management in the Digital Technology Era

Objectives:
  • Understand the nature of e-procurement management
  • Master and understand modern e-procurement systems
  • Understand the requirements needed to establish an e-procurement system
  • Appreciate the principles of negotiation
  • Understand how to implement an e-procurement system
  • Learn how to manage an e-procurement system
Main Axes:
  • Traditional procurement procedures
  • Developing digital procurement management
  • Digital procurement and negotiation models
  • Electronic procurement management
  • Organizational development for electronic procurement management
Enroll in This Program

3. Modern and Advanced Management of Procurement and Contracts

Objectives:
  • In-depth study of the contract negotiation process and the related documents
  • Study of supply contracts and how to manage and conclude contract operations
  • Modern purchasing skills and introduction to the importance of the purchasing function for the organization
  • Awareness of the legal foundations for selecting procurement and supply sources and related skills, in addition to how to obtain information about potential suppliers
  • Skills for determining the economic quantity for purchase and the appropriate purchase price
  • Skills and methods for managing and executing tenders by following specific steps that enable departments to choose the most suitable contractor in supply contracts
  • Provide participants with skills in preparing tenders, bids, and specifications
  • Detailed steps for tender issuance systems and their detailed procedures
  • Awareness of the rules for evaluating bids and analyzing offers submitted for tender award
Main Axes:
  • Advanced procurement management
  • Fundamentals of procurement practices (procurement policies) according to international standards
  • Efficiency of the procurement department's management related to international and local contracting, and its relationship with the planning department and other departments concerned with procurement operations
  • General principles of contract management
  • Mechanisms for monitoring tenders and purchases
  • Legal preparation for procurement negotiation
  • Competence in effective negotiation
  • Negotiation strategies and techniques in procurement
  • Management of tender contracts during the awarding stage
  • Management of procurement tenders
Enroll in This Program

4. Supply Chain Risk Management

Objectives:
  • Determine the level of risk in supply chains
  • Develop an understanding of supply risks and their impact on sustainable business
  • Identify modern concepts of supply chain management and risk as a basis for operational success
  • Plan the supply chain risk management process to align with institutional requirements
  • Identify and evaluate sources of risk in the supply chain
  • Quantitatively and qualitatively assess risks using a versatile set of tools
  • Develop risk mitigation and contingency plans to ensure uninterrupted supply flow
  • Recognize the cultural, ethical, and legal standards to consider when planning supply chain risk management
  • Learn how to conduct risk analysis in supply chains and apply a range of risk management tools and techniques
Main Axes:
  • Supply chain and risk management
  • Planning the risk process in supply chains
  • Identifying supply chain risks
  • Evaluating supply chain risks
  • Responding to supply chain risks
  • Monitoring supply chain risks
Enroll in This Program

5. Effective Management of Procurement Contracts and Claims Assessment and Settlement

Objectives:
  • Introduce the participant to how to manage and conclude contract operations
  • Provide the participant with modern purchasing skills and inform them of the importance of the purchasing function for the organization
  • Awareness of the legal foundations for selecting procurement and supply sources and related skills
  • How to deal with change orders and claims
  • Provide the participant with the skill of determining the appropriate purchase quantity
  • How to obtain information about potential suppliers
  • Confronting potential contract problems during its management
  • How to write contractual terms
Main Axes:
  • Management of direct purchase and supply contracts
  • Modern procurement systems
  • Fundamentals of determining appropriate procurement systems
  • Determining purchase quantities and selecting suppliers
  • Practical problems in contract management
  • Change orders, claims, and their settlement
Enroll in This Program

6. Certified Warehouse Manager

Objectives:
  • Enrich the participants' technical knowledge in supply chain and warehouse management within their work environment, which is concerned with accomplishing tasks to the fullest extent and within a low-cost scope over the long term
  • Inform participants about warehouse operations, methods of classifying, storing, and standardizing materials, managing warehouse operations, and inventory planning
  • Enhance the performance level by developing an integrated concept for warehouse management in supply chains
  • Learn how to increase work accuracy by increasing knowledge of inventory management tasks
  • Learn how to reduce operation costs by understanding and mastering warehouse operation procedures
  • Learn how to raise the organization's level by increasing the professionalism of employees in this field
  • Skills in effectively analyzing, designing, and planning warehouses
  • Applying advanced practices in planning, organizing, and controlling materials and warehouses, and managing storage
  • Study of storage-related risks, the safety tools that must be available in the warehouse, and identifying the most important precautions to avoid these risks and deal with them after they occur
Main Axes:
  • Modern strategies for improving warehouse management
  • Planning and organizing warehouse management in accordance with ISO requirements
  • Models, systems, and methods for inventory control
  • Warehouse management and inventory control
  • Standards of excellence and improvement in inventory lists
  • The advanced Warehouse Management System and Supply Chain LFS 400
